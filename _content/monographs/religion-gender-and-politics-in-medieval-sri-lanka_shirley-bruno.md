> Political instability provided opportunities for royal women, not available to them under the rule of powerful men.

> The early second millennium was a pivotal moment in the history of Theravāda Buddhism.
> Religious reforms carried out at Poḷonnaruva, then-capital of Sri Lanka, shaped the latter course of Buddhism across South and Southeast Asia.
> However, our understanding of these reforms has been over-determined by retrospective accounts written by male monastics, who focused on the heroic deeds of male monarchs.
> This book offers a radical revision of this narrative.
