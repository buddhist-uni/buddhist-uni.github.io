> *rakṣā* texts were inscribed on amulets to safeguard the wearer from illness, danger, and misfortune. These apotropaic manuscripts were written on materials such as birch bark, cloth, or paper, then rolled or folded and enclosed in containers to be carried. [...] Epigraphic and archaeological findings from Central Asia, India, and China provide evidence of their widespread application and ritual use.
