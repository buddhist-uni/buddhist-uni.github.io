> By examining these early Mahāyāna Sūtras and Śāstras, and also early non-Mahāyāna Pāli nikāyas, abhidhammas and commentaries, I argue that *anārambaṇā maitrīkaruṇā* became unique to Mahāyāna because of the fundamental shift of goal from mainstream Buddhism; why *śrāvakas* do not practise *anārambaṇā maitrīkaruṇā* is not originally because of lack of non-conceptual wisdom or lack of understanding of the emptiness of Dharma, but because for *śrāvakas* and mainstream Buddhists, *maitrī* and *karuṇā* are not essential in attaining their bodhi.
>  *Śrāvakas* are those who learn and uphold the teachings taught by the Buddha by actualising the true nature of dharma *dharmatāṃ sākṣātkurvanti*.
