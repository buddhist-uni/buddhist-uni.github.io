> I explore the specifically British history that informs the association between mindfulness and psychological resilience today.
> I show that the association between psychological resilience and mindfulness practice is the result of broader historical concerns about the nature of modern society and psychology.
> Taking a genealogical approach, I argue that changing patterns in British psychology and Buddhism, while framed in universalist registers, are constituted in and constitutive of a broader historical and political context.
