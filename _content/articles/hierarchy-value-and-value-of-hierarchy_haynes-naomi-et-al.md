> Many of the communities in which anthropologists work are hierarchically organized, and the people who live in them often describe this arrangement in positive terms.
> Nevertheless, anthropologists rarely paint hierarchy in a favorable light.

> we explore those aspects of Western thought that make it difficult for anthropologists to take hierarchy seriously.
> In addition, we develop an interpretive approach that treats hierarchy both as a relational form and as a theoretical model
