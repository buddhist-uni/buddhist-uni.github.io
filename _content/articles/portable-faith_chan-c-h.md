> From small-scale shrines to handheld icons and votive tablets, portability has long factored into the design and reception of Buddhist art.


> The article first examines the circulation of miniature icons that served as diplomatic gifts in the sixth and seventh centuries.
> It then turns to figurative plaques from Tang-dynasty China (618–907) that were modified for votive and architectural uses in early Japan.
> Lastly, the article examines the reasons underlying the enduring popularity of portable shrines in the [Japanese] archipelago.
