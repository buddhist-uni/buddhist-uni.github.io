This interview explores David Max Moerman’s study of the largely unknown archive of Japanese Buddhist world maps, analyzing their production, reproduction, and reception. It highlights how his work uncovers an alternative history of Japanese Buddhism—one shaped by a Buddhist geographic imaginary that engaged multiple cartographic and cosmological worldviews.
